LAGATIC

The process of evaluating the work or performance of one's colleagues or peers is referred to as peer evaluation. It is a method by which a collection of individuals on the same team or in the same organization can offer their thoughts and comments regarding the work of other people on the same team or in the same company.

QUESTION 3 - What is the meaning of group dynamics?

The term "group dynamics" refers to the ways in which members of a group or team interact with one another and behave. Group dynamics are the unwritten norms and patterns of behavior that occur inside a group of people. Additionally, group dynamics refer to how these dynamics effect both the group as a whole and the individuals that are a part of the group.

Group dynamics' refers to interplays of people's social cognitions and behaviors operating within a group or between groups. The term was coined by a social psychologist, Kurt Lewin, who established the Group Dynamics Research Center in 1945 at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology.

Group dynamics refers to the way people interact and behave within a group or team. Group dynamics are the unwritten rules and patterns of behavior that occur within a group of people, and how these dynamics affect the group as a whole and the individuals within it.

QUESTION 6

What are the different leadership styles?

  • Autocratic: An autocratic leader makes decisions unilaterally and requires compliance and obedience from their team.
  • Laissez-faire: A laissez-faire leader gives their team members a lot of freedom and autonomy, and provides minimal direction or guidance.
  • Transformational: A transformational leader inspires and motivates their team to achieve a shared vision or goal, and focuses on personal growth and development.
  • Servant: A servant leader places the needs of their team above their own and focuses on serving and supporting others.
  • Transactional: A transactional leader focuses on rewards and punishments to motivate and manage their team.
